It was pretty cool hearing about the different projects that people chose to work on! In particular, it was interesting to see that a group decided to contribute to [p5.js](https://p5js.org/), since it has very strong roots in NYU ITP/IMA, which is the department of my main major. In IMA, we actually have to learn p5.js during the first semester of freshman year in a course called Creative Computing. I don't really use it anymore (I prefer geometry nodes in Blender for that sort of work), but it was an important part of how I first learned to code.
<!--more-->

I remember after the group standups, there were some sentiments expressed in my group about feeling behind other groups. I also remember thinking that I don't really feel that behind other groups, well maybe except for the Keycloak group, since I expected there to be issues with getting the Oppia development environment running. I think that even though it might have seemed like we're behind, this is just as much part of the work of contributing to open source as anything else other people are doing.
